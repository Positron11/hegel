Examples

Examples of the main structure classes Hegel has to offer, and the styling that Argon provides.

Buttons

There are two kinds of buttons (small, regular), and two states (normal, pulse).

Cards

Everything you see here is inside a card. Cards can be stacked, and will take up the maximum possible width.

Insets
You can put things (like this paragraph) into inset grooves, or boxes. Use insets directly inside cards to create a solid effect.
List boxes

List boxes can be used to create brickwork-like grids of small elements. They can have compact or regular spacing, and they can flow vertically or horizontally.

Paragraphs

Paragraphs are formatted to have a 1.5em line-height, for best readability. For text, either use paragraph tags, or apply the text tag. This is an example of a drop-caps paragraph.

Formatting

Text can be formatted as bold, highlight, italic, underlined, small, or a combination of any styles.

Links

Links are formatted like so: This is a link. Visited links are greyed out.

Navbar

You have a choice of two types of navbars. Click the buttons below and scroll to see a demo of each. We handle only the navbar container, what goes inside, and handling responsiveness is up to you - at least, for now.

Images

Autohide Navbar

An autohide hides when srolling down, either after passing its own height, or after passing an element with the navbar_hidepoint id.

Anchors

We take care of anchors for you - smooth scrolling, no url updates, and no worrying about the navbar overlapping the target.
